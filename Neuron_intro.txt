a single neuron (often called a perceptron in its simplest form) is designed to mimic the biological process of taking in signals, weighing their importance, and deciding whether to pass a message along.

Each neuron has inputs + weights + bias.
weights - The weights represent the strength or importance of each input.
- A high positive weight means the input is very important for the output.
- A weight near zero means the input is mostly irrelevant.
- A negative weight means the input actively pushes the result in the opposite direction.

Bias-
it's just like a how to much interest to run it.
- Bias is how easy or hard it is to get the neuron to "fire" before any data even arrives. It sets the "starting line."

Activation function:
Sigmoid function: - yes or no type classification
- used in output layer, Sigmoid is for deciding (it's the final judge).
- it is used to convert any input into [0,1] range o/p as a probability.
- It maps any input into a tight range of (0, 1), which is essential for models that need to interpret output as a probability of belonging to a specific class.

ReLU: Rectified Linear Unit - max(0,x)
- ReLU is for learning (it's the workhorse) - used in hidden layers.
